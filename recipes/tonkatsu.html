Tonkatsu

Tonkatsu (とんかつ, 豚かつ) or deep-fried pork cutlet.

This western-inspired dish has been enjoyed for over 120 years, with the recipe remaining mostly unchanged since then. So, what makes Tonkatsu special, and how can we make this popular dish at home? Believe it or not, making Japanese pork cutlet at home is easier than you think and should take no more than 30 minutes!

What is Tonkatsu?

For those of you who are new to this Japanese dish, tonkatsu consists of tender pork loin (or fillet) that has been breaded and deep-fried to crunchy perfection. The word Ton (豚) comes from “pork” and Katsu (カツ) is an abbreviation of Katsuretu (カツレツ), derived from the English word “cutlet.”

Ingredients

  • Panko Breading
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Tonkatsu Sauce
  • Pork Tenderloin
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Sesame Seeds
  • Rice
  • Cabbage

Cooking Instructions

  1. Tenderize pork and score connective fatty tissue
  2. Mix salt, pepper, flour, and panko on a large tray
  3. Whisk eggs in a large mixing bowl
  4. Heat oil in a deep skillet
  5. Dredge pork in egg wash and then evenly coat in breading
  6. Fry pork in hot oil until goldern brown
  7. Let rest on paper towel to drain excess oil
  8. Serve over rice with shredded cabbage and dipping sauce
  9. Garnish with sesame seeds
